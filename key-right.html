Key Right
Right to Access Mental Healthcare

You have the right to access appropriate mental health services and care from Govt. mental health services, without discrimination, to support your well-being and recovery.

Right to Community Living

You have the right to enjoy living in your community and receive mental health care that respects your independence and dignity. Mental health services should help you remain connected to society, with access to family and community resources. Everyone deserves a community that fosters inclusion and independence.

Right to Protection from Cruel, Inhuman, or Degrading Treatment

You have the right to be treated with respect and dignity, and to be protected from any form of cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment in mental health settings, including all forms of physical, verbal, emotional, and sexual abuse.

Right to Equality and Non-Discrimination

You have the right to access mental health services without discrimination based on age, gender, race, disability, or any other characteristic. Emergency care, ambulance services, and insurance coverage for mental health should be provided with the same urgency and terms as physical health care. Equal access to mental health support is your entitlement.

Right to Information

You have the right to receive clear and accessible information about your mental health, treatment options, and any other services you may need, so you can make informed decisions.

Right to Confidentiality

Your privacy matters. Your personal and medical information should be kept confidential, and shared only with your consent or where ethically or legally required.

Right to Restrictions on Release of Information

Your mental health information is confidential and cannot be shared without your consent, except when required by law. This applied to any media release or digital records of your your personal and treatment details.

Right to Access Medical Records

You have the right to access your mental health records and any information held about your mental health care, unless disclosure is restricted for your safety or the safety of others. If information is withheld, you can request its release through the relevant Board.

Right to Personal Contacts and Communication

While in a mental health facility, you have the right to decide whether to receive visitors, phone calls, and mails, at reasonable times, in line with the facilityâ€™s guidelines. You can also request to limit communication from specific individuals, except for visits and communications from authorized individuals, such as legal representatives, medical professionals responsible for your care, and government officials.

Right to Legal Aid

You have the right to access free legal support to help assert your mental health rights or if you're involved in legal proceedings related to your mental health care. Concerned authorities are required to inform you of this right and provide contact details for legal support.

Note: As a vigilant citizen, your awareness and exercise of these rights help ensure you receive the care and support you deserve. For more detailed information, refer to 18-27 of the Mental Healthcare Act 2017 .
