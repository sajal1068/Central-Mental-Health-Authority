Introduction
About

The Central Mental Health Authority (known as the Central Authority or CMHA) is an autonomous body established by the Central Government under the Mental Healthcare Act, 2017, with the mission to enhance regulated mental healthcare services and ensure quality care across the country. As an advisory body to the Central Government, the Central Authority plays a pivotal role in shaping mental health regulations and service provision standards. It oversees mental health establishments under the Central Government, ensuring adherence to established standards of care and compliance. It is further committed to training and educating stakeholders on the effective implementation of the Mental Healthcare Act.

Led by the Chairperson (Additional Secretary & Mission Director, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Govt. of India), the Central Authority consists of both ex-officio and non-official members, including senior officials from various government departments, directors of National Mental Health Institutes, and experienced mental health professionals such as psychiatrists, clinical psychologists, social workers, and psychiatric nurses. Notably, the Central Authority also includes members with lived experiences of mental illness, caregivers and representatives from NGOs, forming an inclusive team that can accommodate diverse perspectives.

The Central Mental Health Authority Secretariat, overseen by the Chief Executive Officer, is dedicated to providing administrative support and operational excellence to achieve the Authorityâ€™s goals and objectives. The CEO holds administrative control over the officers and employees of the Central Authority and is responsible for implementation of programs, management of financial planning and maintenance of records.

The Central Authority is committed to promoting mental health awareness, improving service delivery, and advocating for the rights and well-being of individuals with mental healthcare needs. It strives to create a supportive environment nationwide where mental health is prioritized, and quality care is accessible to all.

Functions

The Central Authority is dedicated to improving mental healthcare across the country through a range of essential functions:

  • Registration of Mental Health Establishments: The Central Authority grants registration to all mental health establishments under the Central Government, and maintains a comprehensive national register. This register is regularly updated, based on information provided by State Mental Health Authorities.
  • Establishing Quality Standards: The Central Authority develops and implements quality norms and service provision guidelines for various types of mental health establishments, ensuring a minimum standard of care nationwide.
  • Supervision and Complaint Handling: The Central Authority oversees all mental health establishments and actively welcomes feedback from the public. The Authority addresses any reports and complaints about service deficiencies to enhance the quality of care.
  • Maintaining a National Register of Professionals: The Central Authority maintains a national registry of clinical psychologists, mental health nurses, and psychiatric social workers. This list is updated based on information from State Authorities regarding registered mental health professionals.
  • Training and Education: The Central Authority trains a wide range of personnel and stakeholders, to ensure they understand the provisions of the Mental Healthcare Act and can perform their duties, in accordance with the Act, effectively.
  • Advisory Role: The Central Authority advises the Central Government on matters related to mental healthcare and services, contributing to policy development.
  • Additional Functions: The Central Authority also carries out other functions related to mental health as determined by the Central Government, including recognizing establishments previously registered under older laws to ensure a smooth transition to compliance with the current Act.
